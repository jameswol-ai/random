# 🌍 Global Building Standards

This document defines baseline architectural and structural principles applicable across most regions.

---

## 🧱 1. Structural Safety
- Buildings must withstand local maximum expected loads (wind, seismic, snow where applicable)
- Load distribution must follow recognized engineering methods (beam, slab, column logic)
- Redundancy is encouraged in critical structural systems

---

## 🔥 2. Fire Safety Standards
- Minimum 2 evacuation routes for multi-floor buildings
- Fire-resistant materials required for:
  - Stairwells
  - Escape corridors
  - Structural core in high-rise buildings
- Smoke ventilation systems must be included in enclosed structures

---

## 🌬️ 3. Ventilation & Air Quality
- Natural ventilation preferred where climate allows
- Mechanical ventilation required in sealed environments
- Air exchange rate must meet health safety thresholds

---

## 🌱 4. Sustainability Baseline
- Energy-efficient design encouraged (insulation, passive cooling)
- Water reuse systems recommended
- Construction materials should prioritize low environmental impact

---

## 🧭 5. Accessibility
- Barrier-free access for public buildings
- Ramps or lifts required for multi-level access
- Doorways must support mobility devices
